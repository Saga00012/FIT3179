Homicide Statistics

Australian Homicide Statistics in 2022-2023 from the National Homicide Monitoring Program.

There was a total of 232 incidents of homicide recorded by Australia between 1 July 2022 and 30 June 2023. Which was an increase of 14 in comparison to the previous year.

Incident Clearance Rates

In 2022-2023 about 90% of homicide incidents were cleared at the time of reporting. For Australian Capital Territory and Tasmania, 100% were cleared. The remaining states made up for the 10% uncleared cases, with New South Wales having the most un-cleared cases displayed by the bullet chart to the right.

The homicides that are not cleared include incidents where an offender is yet to be indentified or a suspect that has been identified but has not been charged with a homicide offence.

Weapon Use

A weapon was used 86% of the homicide incidents recording in 2022-2023. Where knives and other sharp instruments were the most commonly used weapons in homicides in Australia.

Where since 1989, 35% of homicide incidents have involved knives and sharp instruments. Specifically in 2022-2023 40% of homicides involved a knife or other sharp instrument.

Homicide Classification

The homicide classification is based off the relationship between the primary victim and the primary offender. The confusing classifications are as follows. A domestic homicide was where the victim was the intimate partner or relative of the offender. An acquantaince homicide was where the victim was a friend or knew the offender.

Where domestic homicides was 34% of all homicide incidents, 28% was acquaintance and 15% was stranger. The remaining made up for the remaining 23% where siblicide was the least common homicide incident.
