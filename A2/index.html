Homicide Statistics

Australian Homicide Statistics in 2022-2023 from the National Homicide Monitoring Program.

Homicide Incidents

There was a total of 232 incidents of homicide recorded by Australia between 1 July 2022 and 30 June 2023. Which was an increase of 14 in comparison to the previous year.

Weapon Use

A weapon was used 86% of the homicide incidents recording in 2022-2023. Where knives and other sharp instruments were the most commonly used weapons in homicides in Australia. Where since 1989 35% of homicide incidents have involved this weapon type.

Incident clearance rates

In 2022-2023 about 90% of homicide incidents were cleared at the time of reporting. For Australian Capital Territory and Tasmania, 100% were cleared. The remaining states made up for the 10% uncleared cases, with New South Wales having the most un-cleared cases displayed by the bullet chart.

Homicide Classification

The homicide classification is based off the relationship between the primary victim and the primary offender. Where domestic homicide was 34$ of all homicide incidents, 28% was acquaintance and 15% was stranger.
